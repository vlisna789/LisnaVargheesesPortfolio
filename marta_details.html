MARTA Kiosk Redesign

This is a personal project I took on, where I choose a system I was innately familiar with, conducted a survey, performed an audit and performed a competitive analysis of other similar systems. In conclusion, based on my research, created a whole new flow.

Introduction to Marta

MARTA stands for Metropolitan Atlanta Rapid Transit Authority. MARTA is Atlanta’s main transit system. If you are regular MARTA user, you would purchase a breeze card and reload your breeze card with a MARTA kiosk. The breeze card grants access to the MARTA’s buses and trains, Cobb County Transit System, Gwinnett County System and the GRTA.

Challenges

While designing this system I kept in mind two of my biggest limitations, the physical buttons and the various access options the breeze card gave. Due to the fact that there are so many opportunities for your breeze card, the kiosk can be overwhelming. Not only are there too many options, but there is no priority for certain users, such as those who own a breeze card. The biggest challenge in redesigning this system were the physical buttons. Because the physical buttons were placed evenly on the screen and all equidistant from one other, I had no free choice over where to place the options.

User Research

I created a short 10 question survey about users’ experience with the MARTA system. From that survey I got 34 response and most of the responses were 18-35 years old.

  • 93% of users surveyed use the MARTA trains
  • 83% of users surveyed use the kiosk to reload their card
  • 70% of users surveyed buy either 2 trips or more trips
  • 60% of users surveyed complain that the kiosk lacks efficiency
  • 62% of users surveyed wish they could view the bus schedules on the kiosk
